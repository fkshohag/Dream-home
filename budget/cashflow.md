## 5. Funding sources (typical)

| Source | Typical share | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Own savings | 40–50 % | Liquid cash for early phases |
| House Building Loan (HBL) — Bangladesh House Building Finance Corp / banks | 30–40 % | 10-yr loan @ 9–11 % typical; need title docs + design + estimate |

Plan to have **BDT 12–15 lakh liquid before breaking ground** (covers first 2 months
when contractor wants 25 % paid and no loan disbursement has started).

## 6. Monsoon-aware schedule

If starting in **October 2026** (post-monsoon):

```
Oct '26   Foundation + plinth        (dry, fast)
Nov '26   Columns                    (good weather)
Dec '26   Roof slab                  (dry — critical for slab cure)
Jan '27   Brickwork                  (cool, productive)
Feb '27   Plaster                    (low humidity ideal)
Mar '27   Electrical / plumbing      (interior work)
Apr '27   Doors / windows / tiles    (interior continues)
May '27   Sanitary / paint priming   (interior)
Jun-Aug   ⛔ Monsoon pause           (cure paint, occasional touch-ups only)
Sep '27   Final paint + boundary     (post-monsoon dry)
Oct '27   Handover                   ✓
```

Total: **12 calendar months** with built-in monsoon buffer.

---

## 7. Cash-tight contingency

If a payment milestone is delayed, you can buy time by:

1. **Pause window/door procurement** — they're long-lead but storable. Push back 2 months.
2. **Defer landscape & boundary wall** to a separate Phase-1.5 (saves ~ BDT 4 lakh upfront).
3. **Defer solar** indefinitely — install when budget allows (it's a clean retrofit thanks to the embedded GI sleeves).
4. **Defer roof-garden planting** to year 2 (~ BDT 22,000 saving).

Total potential deferral: **BDT 5–6 lakh** across 4–6 months without affecting structural integrity or move-in date.
