# Vendor List — Bangladesh Suppliers (mid-2026)

*Practical sourcing guide for the dream-home build. Verify current rates by
phoning 2–3 dealers per category.*

---

## 1. Cement

| Brand | Type | Notes | Where to buy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bashundhara Cement** | PPC / OPC | Most common in rural BD; reliable | Local dealer network — every upazila has one |
| **Shah Cement** | OPC | Premium for structural elements | Dhaka + divisional towns |
| Holcim | OPC | Good quality, slightly costlier | Limited rural reach |
| Diamond / Crown | PPC | Mid-tier, OK for plaster + non-structural | Widely available |

**What to specify in your order:** OPC 53-grade for RCC; PPC 43-grade OK for plaster.
Reject any bag older than **3 months** from manufacture date (lumpy / lost strength).

---

## 2. MS rod (rebar)

| Brand | Grade | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **BSRM Xtreme** | Fe-500W | Best quality in BD; most contractors prefer |
| **KSRM** | Fe-500W | Equivalent to BSRM; slightly cheaper |
| AKS | Fe-500W | Mid-tier |
| Anwar | Fe-500 | OK for non-critical work |

**Always demand mill test certificate (MTC).** Reject if not provided.
Common diameters: T8, T10, T12, T16 in 12 m lengths. Order 5 % extra
for lap splicing + waste.

---

## 3. Brick

| Type | Where |
|---|---|
| **1st class bricks** (M-class, 9.5 × 4.5 × 2.75") | Local brickfield — pick one with 5+ years history |
| Auto-bricks (machine-made hollow) | Costlier ~BDT 18/each but lighter & uniform — only if available locally |
| Concrete blocks | Not commonly used rurally; harder to source |

**How to check quality**: drop one brick from chest height on a hard surface — 1st class should not crack. Soak in water 24 h; if it gains > 15 % weight, reject.

---

## 4. Sand & stone

| Material | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Sylhet coarse sand** | Sylhet rivers, transported by truck | Best for RCC — high silica, low silt |
| Local fine sand (river bed) | Local — within 20 km | For plaster + masonry mortar |
| **Stone chips ¾"** | Sylhet boulder source | For RCC; demand consistent sieve size |
| Brick chips ¾" | Local | For PCC + plinth concrete only |

**Silt test for sand**: jar test — sand should have < 6 % silt content. More than that, reject.

## 7. Floor & wall tile

| Brand | Notes |
|---|---|
| **RAK Ceramics** | Premium — vitrified, homogeneous |
| **Akij Ceramics** | Mid-tier — wide range, good quality |
| **Mir Ceramics** | Mid-tier; locally made; cheaper |

## 11. Paint

| Brand | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Berger** | Best in BD; weather-coat + emulsion full range |
| **Asian Paints** | Equivalent quality; good warranty |
| Nippon | OK; cheaper |
| Robialac | Local; cheaper still — OK for non-critical surfaces |

Always go for **weather-coat (acrylic exterior)** for outside and **silk-finish emulsion** for inside walls.

---

## 12. Granite & stone (for kitchen counter)

| Source | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Mirpur granite market (Dhaka)** | Widest selection — imported granite from India, China |
| Local quarry-cut granite (Comilla, Rajshahi) | Cheaper, limited colours |

Standard kitchen counter granite: 20–25 mm thick, polished edge. **Green-grey "Hassan Green"** or **"Black Galaxy"** are popular dark-tone choices.

---

## 13. Hardware (general)

Any divisional town has a hardware bazaar (*hardware doken*). Specifically:
- **Tipu Sultan Road, Dhaka** — biggest in country, but Dhaka prices
- **Khatungonj, Chittagong** — best for stainless-steel hardware
- **Local upazila bazaar** — for basic nails, hinges, locks, GI pipes

## 15. Engineering & professional services

| Service | Suggested rate (BDT) |
|---|---:|
| Architect for design review | 15,000 – 25,000 LS |
| Structural engineer (drawings + supervision) | 35,000 – 60,000 LS |
| Site engineer (0.5 day/week × 10 months) | 80,000 – 1,20,000 LS |
| Surveyor (boundary check) | 5,000 LS |
| Auger soil test (3 holes × 10 ft) | 8,000 – 12,000 LS |

**Tip**: hire the structural engineer who STAMPS the drawings — it's the same person you want supervising the foundation pour. Don't split these roles.

## 16. Approvals

| Approval | Where | Typical fee |
|---|---|---|
| Land mutation (if not done) | Local Tahsil office | 5,000 – 15,000 |
| Building plan approval | Union Parishad (rural) — simpler than RAJUK/CDA | 5,000 – 15,000 |
| Electrical connection | Local REB office | 12,000 – 18,000 |
| Net-metering (if solar) | REB office, post-installation | 2,000 |

---

*All vendors above are common in mid-2026 Bangladesh. Verify current pricing
and availability before procurement. For urgent help, ask any practicing
civil engineer in your district — most upazila headquarters have at least 3.*
